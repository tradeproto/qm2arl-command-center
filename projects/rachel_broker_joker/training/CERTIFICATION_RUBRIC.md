# Certification Rubric — Joker Broker Protocol™

**Status:** Skeleton — finalize after first internal certification run (Day 30)  
**Visibility:** Invitation-only  
**Rule:** Pass/fail. No participation certificates.

---

## Certification Philosophy

Certify operators who:

- End calls early without permission  
- Enforce boundaries under social pressure  
- Hold silence when discomfort appears  
- Kill weak deals without escalating to Rachel  

Fewer certifications = stronger signal. At least one rejection should feel uncomfortable.

---

## Prerequisites (Before Exam)

- [ ] Invited by Rachel or certified referrer — no cold entry  
- [ ] Read public book (Part I minimum)  
- [ ] Completed observation period (Weeks 6–7) with real conversations  
- [ ] No active “free consulting” behavior observed during observation  

---

## Evaluation Dimensions

| Dimension | Pass indicator | Fail indicator |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| **Discovery discipline** | Stops at 15 min; facts only | Advises, explains, or drifts |
| **Boundary language** | Uses default lines verbatim | Softens, apologizes, or customizes into weakness |
| **Silence** | Tolerates pause after boundary | Rescues conversation |
| **Kill decision** | Kills without Rachel approval | Escalates weak deals or seeks reassurance |
| **Protocol gate** | Does not explain protocol to unqualified inbound | Pitches, teaches, or “helps them understand” |

Score each dimension: **Pass** · **Borderline** · **Fail**

**Certify:** All Pass, no Fail  
**Borderline:** Remediation run — one repeat scenario only  
**Fail:** No certification. May re-invite after 90 days at Rachel’s discretion.

---

## Exam Format (Draft)

1. **Simulated discovery call** (15 min) — evaluator plays drifting counterparty  
2. **Silence scenario** — boundary line + 30-second pause; candidate must not rescue  
3. **Kill scenario** — structurally weak commodity deal; candidate must end  
4. **Escalation trap** — “Can you ask Rachel?” — candidate must enforce gate
