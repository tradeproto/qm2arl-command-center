# Style Guide — Joker Broker Protocol (Book)

---

## What This Book Is

The **only public artifact** in a quiet-scale system. It establishes authority and filters serious actors. It does **not** replace certification, explain the full protocol, or serve as a training manual.

**Reader:** Commodity traders, desk managers, trade house operators, private investors entering energy and bulk flows, and internal champions who need language for standards — not hype.

**Not for:** Operators who need reassurance, soft landings, or training that apologizes for hard decisions. Sales and commodity work at this level is not for the weak or thin-skinned. The book and the protocol filter accordingly.

---

## Voice

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Direct, operator-to-operator | TED talk enthusiasm |
| Specific (ports, flows, structures) | Vague “mindset” coaching |
| Short sentences under pressure | Long academic paragraphs |
| Authority from field exposure | Credentials and name-dropping |
| Quiet confidence | Launch energy, funnels, urgency |

**Sounds like:** A senior operator who has seen deals die for predictable reasons and is done explaining twice — and done coddling desks that confuse discomfort with disrespect.

---

## Title Ethos — Why *The Kill Discipline*

Rachel chose this title because **sales and commodity training is not for the weak or thin-skinned.**

- Killing a deal early will feel harsh to people who want another chance  
- Silence after a boundary will feel like disrespect to people who fill every pause  
- Certification will reject operators who need coaching wrapped in encouragement  

That is not a bug. It is the filter. Serious desks end deals early because weak structure costs more than a bruised ego. The book names that standard publicly. The protocol enforces it privately.

## Public Bestseller / Private Gate

**Rachel’s insight:** *“The book is not for everyone — but everyone will want to read it.”* Both are true.

| Everyone can… | Not everyone will… |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Buy and read the book | Finish it or apply it |
| Share it, quote it, assign it to desks | Qualify for certification |
| Aspire to “serious desk” status | Operate at protocol level without enforcement |

**“Not for everyone” on the cover increases demand** — curiosity, aspiration, identity, word of mouth. Quiet scale governs the **protocol and Rachel’s calendar**, not book distribution. The book is designed to spread; see [`BOOK_POSITIONING.md`](BOOK_POSITIONING.md) and [`AMAZON_LISTING_COPY.md`](AMAZON_LISTING_COPY.md).

**Voice rule for mass appeal:** Write for the **ambitious reader** who wants to prove they belong — not only for operators already certified. Part II (how trades close) is the bestseller engine; Part I is the filter that sells copies.

| Section | Words | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Preface | 1,500–2,500 | Why free consulting ends |
| Chapter 1 | 4,000–6,000 | Authority reset — philosophy, not scripts |
| Full book (later) | 45,000–65,000 | 8–12 chapters + appendix |

Sweet spot for finished book: **180–240 pages** — long enough for institutional respect, short enough that a desk manager finishes it.

---

## Structure Rules

1. Every chapter opens with **one field observation** — a deal, a call, a kill decision  
2. Every chapter closes with **one line the reader can use Monday morning** — not homework  
3. **Never** publish desk card verbatim, certification rubric, or invitation mechanics in full  
4. Name the standard; do not give away the gate
