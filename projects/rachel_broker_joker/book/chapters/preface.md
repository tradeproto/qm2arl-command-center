I have spent years inside conversations that should have ended in fifteen minutes.

They didn’t. They ran for an hour. Sometimes two. The counterparty learned nothing durable. My team learned that judgment is always available if you stay on the phone long enough. And I learned the most expensive lesson in global trade intelligence: **free consulting is unpaid product discovery.**

*The Kill Discipline* exists because that era is over.

This is not a book for everyone. Sales and commodity work at the level I operate in is **not for the weak or thin-skinned.** You will read about ending calls, killing deals, and enforcing silence — and if that sounds cruel rather than professional, you are not the reader I wrote for. That is intentional.

## The Problem With Being Helpful

Helpfulness feels like service. On a commodity desk, it is often **liability dressed as generosity.**

When you explain how deals work to someone who has not earned structure, you teach them that standards are optional. When you extend a discovery call because the counterparty is charming, you teach your team that drift is acceptable. When you give judgment away to maintain a relationship, you teach the market that your altitude has no gate.

I did all of this. The demand validated the work. The absence of a paywall invalidated the standard.
